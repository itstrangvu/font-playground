Selfcare thoughts lately

Is there anything cozier than soup? Soup or broth makes a delicious and nutritive breakfast in the colder months and it's an ideal lunch on-the-go in a thermos/cup/jar. And if you have to work through lunch, it's a subtle, sippable meal during meetings.

Plain, organic, and live-culture rich yogurt is a natural and softening cleansing mask. It's moisturizing, gently exfoliating, and helps restore our microbial balance (good bacteria). Some people apply cool yogurt to soothe sunburns and I've even heard of using it vaginally to manage yeast infections. Natural, edible skincare!

Food is provocative! It's common to get pushback from friends/family/colleagues when we make personal nutrition changes. I find having a light hearted and easy attitude about my lifestyle encourages other people to tame their reactions. Smile and stay the course.

Self care is a discipline. Effective leaders take care of themselves in order to stay resourced and avoid burnout. Self care includes creating and taking personal space, holding boundaries and being unavailable (even if it's disappointing) sometimes.

Journaling can relieve stress & anxiety, help regulate our emotions, build confidence, improve memory, enhance creativity and clarify progress & growth. A few sentences a day is enough ‐ this can be a 10 minute daily ritual.

Touch strengthens the immune system! Stimulating our touch receptors reduces stress by lowering blood pressure and cortisol levels. Touch can also strengthen team dynamics, enhance learning engagement, and bond people together. Consensually touch (and be touched by) a friend, lover, or healer regularly.

Healing is a custom job - we're all working with our own health history, circumstances, preferences, and goals. The best way to find what works for you is to choose something resonant and be consistent practicing it. Consistency will reveal what works and what doesn't and is the gateway to personal change.

Food is everything we are. It's an extension of nationalist feeling, ethnic feeling, your personal history, your province, your region, your tribe, your grandma. It's inseparable from those from the get⁠–⁠go. ‐ Anthony Bourdain

Your lymphatic system is like a cellular sewage system. Lymphatic Drainage is when the miles of lymphatic tubules in your body collect waste from your tissue and transport it (using lymph fluid) to your blood for elimination. When your lymph isn't flowing waste and toxins can build up and make you sick. The dry skin brush, rebounder, massage and infrared sauna are some of the tools that activate your lymphatic system.

Reading doesn't just keep the mind sharp - getting lost in a good book can strengthen our sense of belonging, expand our world views, and help us connect to others. Research shows that reading fiction improves empathy. That's healing.
